# Peer Reviewer

## Role Key
`evaluator`

## What They Do
- Scores applications assigned to them against published merit criteria (2 CFR 200.205)
- Completes COI attestation before accessing any assigned application
- Writes scoring rationale/narrative for each criterion
- Uses AI factor suggestions as a cognitive aid; writes independent final scores
- Flags conflicts of interest discovered mid-review for Review Lead resolution

## What They Cannot Do
- Cannot access applications not assigned to them
- Cannot view other reviewers' scores before scoring is complete (blind review)
- Cannot advance or reject applications — advisory role only
- Cannot see award financials, obligation data, or amendment history

## Impact
- Merit scores drive which applications advance to award consideration
- Weak rationale creates audit exposure for the Program Officer
- COI failure contaminates the entire panel — forces reassignment and delays
- Directly shapes which organizations receive federal funding

## Access Level
- Scoped strictly to assigned application queue
- Read-only on application content; write on scoring form only
- Cannot see other orgs' data or unassigned applications

## Cares About
- COI clearance — will recuse rather than risk a finding
- Clear, weighted merit criteria from the NOFO
- AI suggestions as time-savers, not replacements for independent judgment
- Review deadlines — late scores block award cycle

## Regulatory Anchors
| Reg | Topic |
|-----|-------|
| 2 CFR 200.205 | Merit review process and criteria |
| 2 CFR 200.318(c) | Conflict of interest in evaluation |
