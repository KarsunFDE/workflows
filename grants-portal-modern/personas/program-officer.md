# Program Officer

## Role Key
`program_manager`

## What They Do
- Owns the Notice of Funding Opportunity (NOFO) — writes it, publishes it, versions it
- Manages the pre-award merit-review process end-to-end (2 CFR 200.204–205)
- Assigns applications to peer reviewers; monitors COI attestations
- Reviews and accepts/rejects AI-generated evaluation factor suggestions (HITL Gate 3)
- Monitors post-award performance reporting deadlines

## What They Cannot Do
- **Cannot sign the Federal award** — that authority belongs to the Grants Management Officer
- Cannot modify another agency's NOFO or applications
- Cannot open OIG audit findings

## Impact
- Controls which applications advance through merit review
- Shapes the scoring rubric and criteria weighting in the NOFO
- Single largest influence on award quality and mission alignment
- Bottleneck if unavailable — merit review stalls without them

## Access Level
- Full read/write on applications assigned to their program
- Manages panel assignments and COI status
- Sees HITL gate status but cannot force-approve gate 1 or 2 without proper authority
- No access to obligation/financial ledger

## Cares About
- Scoring integrity and documented rationale
- COI attestation completeness before any reviewer touches an application
- Timely reviews — delays block award cycles
- AI draft quality at Gate 3; will not rubber-stamp AI suggestions

## Regulatory Anchors
| Reg | Topic |
|-----|-------|
| 2 CFR 200.204–205 | NOFO requirements + merit review |
| 2 CFR 200.206 | Risk review (monitors; Grants Officer owns) |
| 2 CFR 200.328–329 | Post-award performance reporting |
