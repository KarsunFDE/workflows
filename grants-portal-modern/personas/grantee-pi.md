# Grantee / Principal Investigator

## Role Key
`vendor`

## What They Do
- Submits SF-424 grant application on behalf of their organization
- Signs certifications and assurances as authorized organizational representative
- Responds to requests for additional information during eligibility/risk screening
- Files post-award performance and financial reports (2 CFR 200.328–329)
- Requests amendments, no-cost extensions, and budget revisions post-award
- Uses AI narrative drafting to accelerate application writing

## What They Cannot Do
- Cannot see other organizations' applications
- Cannot access agency-internal workflows (merit panel assignments, HITL gates, obligation ledger)
- Cannot approve their own award — purely applicant-side

## Impact
- Quality of submitted application directly determines award eligibility
- Reporting compliance (or lack thereof) affects future eligibility (2 CFR 200.206 risk factor)
- External to the agency — dependent on portal UX for all interaction

## Access Level
- Scoped to their own organization's submissions only
- Sees application status updates (not internal gate decisions)
- Post-award: sees their award package, reporting calendar, and amendment history

## Cares About
- Application form clarity — what fields are required, what format
- Status transparency — why was my application returned?
- AI help writing narrative sections
- Budget revision approval timelines
- Post-award reporting deadlines

## Regulatory Anchors
| Reg | Topic |
|-----|-------|
| 2 CFR 200.206 | Risk review criteria (org must demonstrate stability) |
| 2 CFR 200.308 | Prior approval for budget changes |
| 2 CFR 200.328–329 | Performance and financial reporting |
| SF-424 | Standard application form |
